Hugo vs Jekyll

Developer Problems

As developers we always want to start writing our own blogs and give something back to the community which always has got our backs, but its something that we mostly never get around to (well thats me anyways). Who has time to write your own blogs when you have deadlines and prod bugs. But even if we make up our mind to start writing and clear on of your weekend schedules, there comes The Paradox of Choice medium, ghpages etc etc. The trick is to bite the bullet and choose one and stick to it. I’ll tell you what I did.

3rd party blogging vs doing something on your own

SSG Wars: Jekyll vs Hugo

When we come to SSGs there is no shortage of it we have Python SSGs, Ruby SSGs and possibly one for every programming language ever created. My initial bias was towards python I was looking into (Nikola, Hyde, Pelikan) but to be honest its a bit difficult to configure these although they give you a huge set of features, I didn’t have that requirement and wanted to go with something simple and something with a more aesthetic theming.

Enter Jekyll

Jekyll is one of the most popular SSG with a lot of themeing and additional functionality. Its a great platform for content creators. Although this is purely my opinion but i found some shortcoming as

  • It was a bit slow
  • Was built with ruby and i’ve not worked with ruby before

so I decided to Go against it ( see what i did there ).

Enter Hugo

Hugo was the perfect fit for my requirements and I could see some additional benefits of using it as well

  • Its performant
  • Has a plethora of themes for most common use cases
  • I get to play around with Golang (win-win)
